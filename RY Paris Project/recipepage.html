The Bengal Burger

The Bengal Burger brings bold, vibrant flavor inspired by the spices of South Asia. A juicy, perfectly seasoned patty is layered with crisp vegetables, a cooling yogurt mint sauce, and a warm hint of curry that ties everything together. Every bite balances heat, freshness, and richness, creating a burger that feels both comforting and adventurous. It is a street food style twist on a classic American favorite, crafted to wake up your taste buds.


    Ingredients

    For the Patties
  • 1 lb ground beef (80/20 works best)
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp onion powder
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp black pepper

    • For the Yogurt Mint Sauce
  • ½ cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh mint
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • Pinch of salt

    • Toppings
  • Sliced red onion
  • Sliced tomato
  • Crisp lettuce
  • Pickled cucumbers (optional)

    • Buns
  • 4 toasted brioche or sesame buns

    Instructions

    Mix the patties:
  1. Combine the ground beef with curry powder, cumin, turmeric, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Mix gently until just combined.

    2. Form the burgers:
  2. Shape into 4 even patties, pressing a small indent in the center so they cook evenly.

    3. Cook the patties:
  3. Grill or pan sear over medium high heat for 3 to4 minutes per side, or until they reach your preferred doneness.

    4. Make the yogurt mint sauce:
  4. Stir together yogurt, mint, lemon juice, and a pinch of salt. Chill until ready to use.

    5. Toast the buns:
  5. Lightly butter and toast the buns until golden.

    6. Assemble the burgers:
  6. Spread yogurt mint sauce on the bottom bun, add the patty, then layer with onion, tomato, lettuce, and pickles if you like.

    7. Serve:
  7. Top with the remaining bun and enjoy the warm, spiced, fresh flavor combo.
